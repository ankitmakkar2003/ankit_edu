What You'll Learn in 6 Weeks

WEEK 1

Foundational Principles Of Biomechanics

Understand what most don't know about biomechanics and how you can use these principles to guide movement.

WEEK 2

Neurological Influences on Movement

Uncover how the nervous system impacts movement and performance.

WEEK 3

Functional Anatomy Of The Upper Body

Develop an understanding of breathing, scapular and thoracic mechanics, and the relevant muscles and joints that are associated with assessment and exercise selection.

WEEK 4

Functional Anatomy Of The Lower Body

Dive deep into lower body mechanics such as the foot, ankle, hip and pelvis, and how they relate to movement, categorising exercises and assessment.

WEEK 5

Assessment

Learn how to assess to be able to pick the best exercise to maximize results.

WEEK 6

Program Design

Learn the art of designing purposeful exercise programs, tailoring them to optimise movement and performance.

Frequently Asked Questions 

What is the time commitment needed?

The time commitment for our Foundational Biomechanics Course is designed to be flexible and accommodating. Over the course of 6 weeks, we'll deliver engaging 60-90 minute worth of video modules each week, allowing you to delve into the material at your own pace. You have the freedom to speed up or slow down your learning journey based on your preferences and schedule. Plus, with a generous 1-year access period, you can revisit and reinforce your knowledge whenever you need to. Whether you're eager to complete the course in the suggested time or prefer to spread your learning out, this course is tailored to fit your unique needs and ensure a positive learning experience.

What is the difference between the EVOLVE Mentorship and the Foundation Course?

The Foundations Course was designed as an entry-level course into Alex Effer's perspective on Biomechanics. It goes over all of the basics that you would want to know regarding movement, functional anatomy and assessment. This course acts as a great prequel to The EVOLVE Biomechanics Mentorship. It covers foundational concepts that are the base information for EVOLVE.  Additionally, The Foundations Course is self-paced vs. an active Mentorship like EVOLVE; where we have live tutorials each week, community groups and learning pods to further discuss the concepts. The EVOLVE Mentorship also dives deeper into practical applications and more advanced Biomechanics concepts.  

Is this course only for beginners?

Our Foundations Biomechanics Course is designed to cater to a wide range of individuals, making it ideal for beginners who are just starting their journey in understanding movement concepts. However, it's not just limited to beginners. Professionals in the health and fitness industry who are looking for a comprehensive review of movement concepts will also find immense value in this course. This course will also provide you access to Alex Effer's perspective on the body and movement, gaining valuable insights that can enhance your understanding of biomechanics and assessment.

How long will I have access to the course content?

You'll lifetime access to the course content! We want to make sure you have ample time to explore the material, revisit it as needed, and enjoy a flexible and positive learning experience at your own pace.

I plan on taking EVOLVE, can they be bundled together?

Absolutely they can! The intention of this Foundations Course was to act as a prequel to The EVOLVE Mentorship. Based on the feedback and questions asked during EVOLVE, we have put this Course together to go over the basics of movement, the body, functional anatomy, assessment and program design. These concepts will be integral for EVOLVE as we use it as the foundations for the material covered.

What if I have questions about the material?

Our Foundations Biomechanics Course is designed to be self-guided, allowing you to independently progress through the material at your own pace. As you watch the video lessons, you'll have the opportunity to ask general questions directly below each video and also see the questions that have been asked by other students. Your instructor, Alex Effer, will be monitoring the comments section and will respond to your questions in a timely manner. If you are looking for more in-depth support and clarification from Alex himself, we encourage you to Join the VAULT Community Learning Group. This Learning Group is exclusive to those who have taken one of the Resilient Education courses or mentorships. It gives you access to a group of amazing, like-minded professionals, bi-weekly Zoom Q/A calls and a community form where you can post questions and get feedback!
